Hello! My name is Jasper Palmer. I am a mechanical engineering student at the University of British Columbia. I have loved aviation since I was 6 years old, and I eventually hope to be an aeronautical engineer working on designing aircraft. To this end, I am enrolled in the Aerospace concentration of mechanical engineering, with my electives focusing on important aerospace topics such as CFD, propulsion, and aircraft design. However, throughout my degree I have worke don interesting projects and learned about topics which are foundational to this.

In first year, I helped design a unique solution to a humanitarian problem by utilizing plastic barrels combined with filters to make the best of situations where walking to local unclean wells is required. I also helped to design a practical solution to a Canadian problem in the form of a set-it-and-forget-it solution to protecting property from wildfires, that would automatically detect nearby fires and soak the surrounding area.

In second year, I helped program a self-driving car to follow a racetrack without programming the path, instead utilizing a camera which recorded live video which then was applied to a 4 layer neural network which regressed the photo to a steering angle output. Additionally, over the summer I earned my PPL in the US.

In third year, I spent a semester abroad at TU Delft in the Masters of Aerospace Engineering program. At TU Delft I designed a coupled Westland Lynx helicopter stability model of coupled differential equations. I also helped program a small quadcopter to fly around a given space while avoiding static and moving obstacles.

In fourth year, I helped design a hydrogen storage solution for a zero emission locomotive for my Capstone project.
