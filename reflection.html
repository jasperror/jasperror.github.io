What did you learn about the design process from your experience in this course?

Working on a hydrogen storage solution for a zero-emission locomotive reinforced that the engineering design process is highly iterative, where continuous refinement through testing and feedback is essential. Defining the problem in the context of decarbonizing rail transport and understanding stakeholder needs—such as safety regulators, operators, and the public—were critical to developing a viable solution. Teamwork and communication were vital when integrating mechanical, safety, and energy considerations. Time management and planning proved crucial given system complexity. Prototyping and failure provided key insights, especially around safety and feasibility. Real-world constraints, including cost, materials, and sustainability, shaped decisions. Documentation ensured accountability, while adaptability allowed us to respond to evolving technical and regulatory challenges.

Based on your experiences in this course, what are the most important attributes of an engineer?

This project emphasized that strong engineers combine technical expertise with adaptability and curiosity, particularly when working on emerging technologies like hydrogen energy systems. Problem-solving skills are essential for tackling complex, open-ended challenges tied to sustainability and infrastructure. Communication and teamwork are critical when collaborating across disciplines and with stakeholders. Time management and accountability ensure progress on large-scale projects. Resilience is key when addressing failures in design and testing. Ethical awareness is especially important when dealing with safety-critical systems and environmental impact. Ultimately, creativity and continuous learning enable engineers to contribute to innovative, low-carbon solutions.

How has your experience of this project (or in other courses) contributed to your understanding of the role and responsibilities of the professional engineer in society?

This project highlighted that engineers play a key role in advancing sustainable technologies that address global challenges like climate change. Designing a hydrogen storage system for a zero-emission locomotive emphasized responsibilities related to public safety, environmental stewardship, and regulatory compliance. It reinforced the importance of accountability, transparent communication, and designing systems that are not only innovative, but also safe, reliable, and socially responsible.

Based on what you learned about yourself in this course and over your time in the SoE, what strengths do you bring to your engineering career? Which areas need growth or development, and how will you facilitate that growth?

I bring strong analytical thinking, persistence, and the ability to collaborate effectively in multidisciplinary teams tackling complex problems. I remain dependable under pressure and receptive to feedback, which supports iterative improvement. I aim to further develop technical depth in energy systems and build confidence in leadership and decision-making. I plan to do this through continued learning, seeking mentorship, taking on leadership roles, and reflecting on feedback to strengthen both technical expertise and professional judgment.
